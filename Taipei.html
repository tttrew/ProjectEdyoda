Taipei, Taiwan

Taipei presents to us the the definition of a perfect city. Its intense futuristic look, created by its
various skyscrapers and infrastructure, mixes perfectly with its old-school buildings reflecting its culture.
These both combine flawlessly to give Taipei its quite stunning skyline.

The stunning capital city of Taiwan will give you no time to rest in your hotel room, with its various
and varied attractions that cannot simply miss out on, its four main museums: first being the National Taiwan
Museum contains records of the growth of Taiwan from a colony of Japan to where it is now. The National
palace museum is no exception as well, containing ancient Chinese artifacts dating back to almost 7000
years.The Shung Ye Museum of Formosan Aborigines stands just 200 metres away from the National Taiwan Museum
displays a range of Taiwanese art dating back to almost the origins of the people of Taiwan. Finally is the
Taiwan Fine Arts Museums, the one and only museum in Taiwan devoted to the study of modern art, The museum is
home to almost three thousand pieces of brilliant mordern art sorted into thirteen different categories. Yet
these are nothing when compared to the jewel in the crown...

The jewel in the Crown of Taipei is none other than the Taipei 101, just laying eyes on this man-made giant
will simply take your breath away. Opened in 2004 it was the tallest structure made by man until the
building of the Burj Khalifa. The building has a massive height of 509 metres and is the highlight of the
skyline of Taipei. Just visit on New Year's Eve and you will be stunned by its annual firework display.

The City of Taipei is too compelling, you just have to visit it!
