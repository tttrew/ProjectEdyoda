The Great Pyramids of Giza

They were the tallest ever man-made structure for close to 4700 years. Yet the still stand as strong and as
gigantic as ever. The Great Pyramids of Giza are structures that have stood on the Giza Plateau for most of
Human History yet one is always in awe looking at them. The sheer volume and size of these great tombs may
leave one speechless during their first visit, for what can someone say about such greatness!

Standing at a massive height of 490 feet, with all its four sides facing the cardinal directions of the
compass at an accuracy 1/20th of a degree, these grand structures show us the power and
skillfullness of our egyptian ancestors. The pyramids might be the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the ancient
world yet it remains the only one still standing, indisputedly making it the greatest of the seven.

The Pyramid was to remain sealed since its finishing, this was not the case, today tourists can enter and
explore the pyramids, through the aptly named 'Thieves entrance' which through a narrow tunnel
takes us to the stunning Grand Gallery, grand for the beautiful hieroglyphs engraved and carved into its
walls. The grand gallery takes us to the King's Chamber, which is a simple room consisting of an empty
sarchophagus.

The pyramid has stood for 4700 years and will continue to stand, for man fears time but even time fears the
pyramids.
